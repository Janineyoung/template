DESIGN CONCEPT

It is a system that helps stay-at-home dads come back to professional industry. The system contains two parts: Online Community - A website aiming to engage stay-at-home dads to people from professional industry as well as provide them possible job opportunities. Offline Community - A Tie Clip with a NFC Chip that contains the owner’s professional information. When the dad taps his Tie Clip to a recruiter’s phone, his information (with LinkedLn URL) will go directly to the phone, in this way, the tie clip allows for physical connection between people - especially between stay-at-home dads and industry professionals.

DESIGN PROCESS

The whole Design Process is divided into four parts. We started from defining our targeted users and understanding their pain points. Then we finally decided that we wanted to solve the serious problem which is their difficulty in coming back to professional industry. After brainstorming, we came up with three design solutions. Then, the final design idea was deciced and prototype was developed. The last step was evaluation. We set our evaluation plan and conducted different types of evaluations to analyze our prototype.


Part 1 - Topic Definition & Understanding the Problem

View Report1

In this part, we conducted literature review on targeted demographic and also on the problem they are faced with. Through the literature review, we found that stay-at-home dads are confronted trouble when they want to come back to the professional industry. We had deeper understanding of the trouble by surveys and case study of a stay-at-home dad who wants to go to the industry in the following years. Then we analyzed existing similar systems like LinkedIn, Facebook by conducting task analysis. These systems provide us some design implications to be implemented further. And we found that there is no product that have the same target as we do, implying that we have a vacant market for our project.


Part 2 - Design Alternatives

Part 3 - System prototype and evaluation plan

View Report3

In this part, we developed our prototypes - both website and the hardware device. Both were mid-fidelity prototypes that could be evaluated. We described our prototypes including the iteration, implementation challenge, design justification, task analysis, and usability specification in the report. And we also came up with our evaluation plan for next step-Evaluation.


Part 4 - Evaluation

View Report4

In this part, we conducted our product evaluation. We chose to use three methods of evaluation: an expert review based on the heuristics informing the usability specifications we used to design the system, an automated inspection to measure conformance to web accessibility guidelines, and a user study to gauge the responses of representative participants. The data we collected provided many positive feedbacks and also the possible future directions.






DESIGN REFLECTIONS


User reach out

We only reached out our target users at the very beginning and the end of the design process. We failed to kept in touch with them and connected them during the design process. I would asked for their opinions and feedbacks during the whole process so that we can have better rationale as well as solutions for the design and deeper understanding of the problem.

Iteration

The whole process is just one iteration of the product. However, I would like to conduct more iterations as we have already got so many future directions. The future iteration would make a lot of progress and is worth conducting.
